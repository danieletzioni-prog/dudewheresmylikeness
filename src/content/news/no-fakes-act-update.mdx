A federal right-of-publicity-adjacent statute for digital replicas would solve
the most obvious problem with the current landscape: state-by-state fragmentation.
It would also, depending on how it's drafted, **preempt** the stronger state
statutes — California's AB 2602, Tennessee's ELVIS Act — that the industry actually
fought for.

The current draft language tries to thread the needle by setting a federal floor
while explicitly preserving state law. Legal scholars are skeptical that framing
survives the first serious preemption challenge. If it doesn't, the federal bill
becomes a ceiling, not a floor — which would be a meaningful step *backward* for
performers in the states that did the early work.

Worth watching closely. The details here will matter far more than the headlines.
