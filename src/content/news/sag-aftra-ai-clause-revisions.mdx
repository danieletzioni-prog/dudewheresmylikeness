The AI digital replica protections SAG-AFTRA won in the 2023 strike were,
correctly, framed as a consent regime: no replica without specific, informed,
revocable consent. That framing held for about eighteen months.

The emerging revisions — being drafted now, ahead of the next negotiating
cycle — are shifting the center of gravity toward **compensation structure.**
Consent is still a floor, but the real fight is over whether replica use gets
priced as a buyout, a residual, or a per-use royalty. Each structure implies a
very different relationship between talent and their digital selves.

The short version: a buyout model treats a replica like a recording. A residual
model treats it like a syndicated performance. A per-use royalty model treats
it like a software license. Which one wins will shape the economics of
below-the-line work for the next decade.
